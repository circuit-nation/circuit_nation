# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Motorsport fans across the entire spectrum — from first-timers who just watched their first F1 race to obsessives who can recite every constructor championship since 1958. Includes sim racers who blur the line between watching and competing, and F1/MotoGP fans who live for live discussion, community threads, standings drama, and engineering deep-dives. They come here to feel at home, to find their people, and to never feel like they're watching alone.

## Product Purpose

Circuit Nation is the fan-built hub for everything motorsports — Formula 1, MotoGP, sim racing, and the engineering obsession behind it all. It exists because official channels are corporate and fan communities are fragmented. Success means a motorsport fan landing on this page and immediately feeling "this was made for me." The CTA is joining the community; everything on the landing page earns that conversion by making the product feel unmissably alive.

## Brand Personality

Passionate, inclusive, loud. The energy of a packed grandstand, not a press conference. It celebrates every fan voice — the purist, the newcomer, the sim racer — without hierarchy. It's the friend who texts you mid-race with the hot take you already had.

## Anti-references

- **ESPN / Sky Sports**: Busy sports-news portal with no visual hierarchy — too many stories competing, zero sense of community.
- **Generic SaaS landing pages**: Feature grid + clean hero + big CTA — fine for productivity tools, completely wrong register for a fan community.
- **Reddit / Discord**: Functional and utilitarian but uninspiring — no visual ambition, no sense that the platform itself cares.
- **Official F1 / MotoGP sites**: Corporate, heavily team-branded, product-catalog energy — serves sponsors, not fans.

## Design Principles

1. **Alive, not archived.** The landing page should feel like something is happening *right now* — live counts, race tickers, real community energy. Static "about us" energy kills it.
2. **Fan-built confidence.** The aesthetic earns credibility through specificity: real race names, real community numbers, real motorsport texture. Vague "passion" copy is the opposite.
3. **Loud without being noisy.** Big, expressive, energetic — but with clear hierarchy. Every screen element knows its rank and fights for attention in order.
4. **Broad tent, one voice.** From casual to obsessive, everyone sees themselves here. No gatekeeping jargon, no dumbing down — the tone assumes intelligence and shared enthusiasm.
5. **The platform has a pulse.** Community is the product. Design choices (motion, real-time data, social proof, testimonials) should make that community feel tangible, not theoretical.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target specified. Maintain reasonable contrast on body text, ensure focus states exist, and respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (already implemented in the codebase). Broad audience inclusivity matters more than formal compliance here.
